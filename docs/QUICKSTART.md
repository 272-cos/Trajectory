# Quick Start - First Time User

**You just opened the app. Here's what to do in 2 minutes.**

## Step 1: Create Your Profile (30 seconds)

1. Tap **Profile** tab at the top
2. Enter your **birthday** (use the date picker)
3. Select **Male** or **Female**
4. Tap **Copy Profile Code** button
5. Paste it in your Notes app

Save this code. You will need it to reload your profile on another device.

## Step 2: Record Your First Workout (60 seconds)

1. Tap **Self-Check** tab
2. Enter today's date
3. Fill in what you did:

   **Pick one cardio:**
   - 2-mile run - enter time like `13:30`
   - HAMR - enter shuttles like `94`
   - 2km walk - only if you have a profile

   **Pick one strength:**
   - Push-ups - how many in 1 minute
   - Hand-Release Push-Ups - how many in 2 minutes

   **Pick one core:**
   - Sit-ups - how many in 1 minute
   - Reverse Crunches - how many in 2 minutes
   - Plank - time like `3:45`

   **Body comp (always required):**
   - Height in inches
   - Waist in inches (measured at belly button)

4. Watch the score at the top update as you type
5. Tap **Save Assessment** when done

## Step 3: What Just Happened?

You now have:
- **Profile Code** (demographics) - your age and gender, used for scoring
- **Assessment Code** (self-check) - your workout results for this session

Your **score estimate** shows at the top. This is NOT an official score, just an estimate.

## Sharing and Moving Between Devices

After saving an assessment, tap **Share / QR** to:
- Display a QR code the other device can scan to load your data automatically
- Or tap "Share via Device" to send the link through your phone's share sheet
- Or copy the link manually

No manual code pasting required on the receiving side - opening the link loads your data.

## That's It!

Next time you work out, repeat Step 2. Each session is stored separately so you can track your trend over time.

## What About the Other Tabs?

**History** - See all your past assessments in a timeline with trend charts.
Tap any entry to expand it and see the breakdown. Use the "Backup JSON" button
to download all your data as a file for safekeeping.

**Trajectory** - Enter your next PFA date to see if you are on track. Set a
personal score goal (anywhere from 75 to 100) to aim higher than the minimum.
The days-remaining counter turns amber inside 90 days and red inside 30 days
so the urgency is always visible.

**Tools** - Includes a "What Score Do I Need?" lookup. Drag the slider to any
target composite and see exactly what raw performance you need per component -
broken down by exercise. Also has a HAMR metronome and stopwatch.

**Report** - Generate a formatted text report for your supervisor.

## Dark Mode

Tap the moon icon in the top-right corner of the header to switch to dark mode.
Your preference is saved and persists across sessions.

## What if I Have a Medical Profile?

If you have an AF Form 469 exempting you from a component:
1. In Self-Check, toggle the **Exempt** switch for that component
2. Skip the data entry for that component
3. Your score will calculate from the remaining components

## What if I'm at High Altitude?

Only matters if you're at one of these bases:
- USAF Academy
- Schriever SFB
- Cheyenne Mountain SFS
- F.E. Warren AFB
- Peterson SFB
- Buckley SFB
- Kirtland AFB

If you are:
1. Toggle "Are you at a base in CO, WY, or NM?"
2. Select your base from the dropdown

## Common Questions

**Q: Where is my data stored?**
A: Only in your browser. No server. No cloud. Just your device.

**Q: What if I lose my codes?**
A: Use the "Backup JSON" button in History to download all your data as a file.
Keep that file somewhere safe. Or share codes to a second device using the Share / QR button.

**Q: Can I use this on multiple devices?**
A: Yes. Use Share / QR from the Self-Check or History tab, or paste codes manually.
The link that opens on the other device loads your data automatically.

**Q: Is this an official Air Force app?**
A: NO. This is unofficial. Always defer to your UFPM and DAFMAN 36-2905.

**Q: Will this score match my official PFA?**
A: Probably close, but maybe not exactly. Official scores come from FACs only.

**Q: What is the personal goal for?**
A: The Trajectory tab lets you set a goal above the 75.0 passing threshold - say
85 or 90 - so your projection shows whether you are on track for your ambition,
not just compliance.
